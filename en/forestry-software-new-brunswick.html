    Streamline your wood basket operations from harvest to reforestation

    New Brunswick's forestry industry is a cornerstone of the provincial economy, employing thousands of workers across a landscape where the forest is never far away. With approximately 85% of the province covered by forest, New Brunswick operates a unique mix of Crown timber licences and private woodlot management that creates distinct challenges for forestry software New Brunswick operators need. From large-scale industrial operations managed by major licence holders to the province's extensive network of private woodlot owners, the demand for efficient digital tools has never been greater. This guide explores how purpose-built logging software NB operations can rely on transforms productivity, compliance and profitability.

    New Brunswick's forestry context

    New Brunswick's forest industry operates under a Crown timber licence system that allocates harvesting rights on public land to major licensees. These licensees are responsible for sustainable forest management, including harvesting, road construction, silviculture and regeneration. The province is also home to one of Canada's largest concentrations of private woodlot owners, with tens of thousands of individual lots contributing a significant share of the province's annual wood supply.

    The mixed-wood character of New Brunswick's forests -- combining softwood species like spruce, fir and pine with hardwoods such as maple, birch and poplar -- means that operations must track production across a wider variety of species than in purely boreal regions. This diversity adds complexity to scaling, pricing, production reporting and delivery management, all areas where forest management New Brunswick companies benefit enormously from integrated software solutions.

    Private and Crown land management challenges

    One of the defining features of New Brunswick forestry is the interplay between Crown land and private land operations. Many contractors work on both Crown timber licences and private woodlots, sometimes in the same week. Each land type has its own regulatory framework, reporting requirements and pricing structures. Managing these parallel systems with paper-based processes leads to confusion, duplication of effort and errors.

    ForestLogix handles multi-client and multi-site management natively, allowing contractors to maintain separate configurations, price lists and reporting templates for each land type and each client. Production data, timesheets and delivery records are automatically categorized and routed to the correct reporting stream, eliminating the manual sorting that consumes hours of administrative time each week.

    How G.A. Logix tools help NB operators

    G.A. Logix solutions address the full spectrum of challenges that New Brunswick forestry companies face. ForestLogix serves as the central management platform, handling everything from job site setup and prescription management to production tracking, fleet maintenance and client reporting. The system is designed for the realities of forestry: it works with the complex pricing structures that characterize NB operations (different rates by species, product, destination and season) and generates detailed reports in the formats that licensees and government agencies require.

    MobileLogix puts data collection directly in the hands of field crews. Running on rugged tablets that withstand New Brunswick's variable climate -- from deep winter cold to humid summer heat -- the application captures timesheets, fuel logs, production volumes and GPS data entirely offline. Crew members enter data once, at the point of activity, eliminating the transcription errors and delays that plague paper-based systems.

    The real-time synchronization provided by SyncLogix ensures that data collected in the field reaches the office without delay. The moment a tablet connects to Wi-Fi at camp or picks up a cellular signal, all data flows bidirectionally: field data uploads to ForestLogix, while updated prescriptions and instructions download to the tablet.

    Reforestation tracking with TrackLogix

    New Brunswick has significant reforestation and silviculture programs, both on Crown land (where regeneration is a licence obligation) and through provincial programs that support private woodlot management. Tracking planting progress, planter productivity and planted area coverage has traditionally been a manual, error-prone process.

    TrackLogix transforms reforestation tracking in New Brunswick by using smartwatch GPS to monitor every planter in real time. The system automatically records planting tracks, calculates planted acreage and generates production reports without any paperwork. Supervisors see a live dashboard showing each planter's position, speed and progress against daily targets. For silviculture contractors working across multiple NB planting sites, TrackLogix provides the accountability and documentation that clients and government programs demand.

    Bilingual interface: critical for NB's workforce

    New Brunswick is Canada's only officially bilingual province, and this reality is deeply embedded in the forestry workforce. Crews in the Acadian regions of northern and eastern New Brunswick are predominantly francophone, while operations in the southern and western parts of the province are primarily anglophone. Many companies employ crews that span both linguistic communities.

    G.A. Logix solutions feature a fully bilingual French/English interface that allows each user to work in their preferred language. This is not a superficial translation -- every form, report, menu and help text is available in both languages. For New Brunswick forestry companies, this bilingual capability is not a nice-to-have but an operational necessity that reduces training time, prevents data entry errors and ensures every crew member can use the tools effectively.

    Integration with existing GIS workflows

    New Brunswick forest engineers and planners use GIS extensively for forest management planning, harvest scheduling and spatial analysis. SIGLogix enables seamless integration between field data and GIS workflows by supporting export to QGIS and ArcGIS in industry-standard formats (Shapefile, GeoJSON, GeoPackage). GPS tracks, block boundaries and field observations collected with MobileLogix flow directly into existing GIS projects. Planners can also prepare spatial data layers in their GIS environment and push them to field tablets, creating a bidirectional workflow that keeps field operations aligned with management plans.
